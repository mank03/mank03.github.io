About University of Guelph

The University of Guelph is a renowned public university known for its strong focus on learning, innovation, and community involvement. Since its founding in 1964, the university has offered a wide range of programs in areas like agriculture, veterinary medicine, engineering, and the arts and sciences. It's especially recognized for its research in areas such as environmental sustainability and food security. With a welcoming campus and diverse student body, the University of Guelph is dedicated to fostering critical thinking, creativity, and collaboration, all while making a positive impact and improving life.

Communications and Computing Services (CCS) at the University of Guelph is the central IT department responsible for providing essential technological support and services across the campus. CCS plays a crucial role in ensuring that the university's IT infrastructure, including networks, email, and cloud services, runs smoothly and securely. They support teaching, research, and administrative functions by offering a wide range of services. In addition to maintaining the university‚Äôs IT systems, CCS is also involved in implementing innovative projects that enhance the university's digital landscape, including the Research Ethics Online System (EthOS). CCS is dedicated to providing reliable and cutting-edge technology solutions that meet the evolving needs of the University of Guelph community.
